<p><ul><li>CCD</li><li>CMOS</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Cameras for microscopes primarily utilize two types of sensors: CCD (Charge-Coupled Device) and CMOS (Complementary Metal-Oxide-Semiconductor). CCD cameras are known for their high image quality and sensitivity, making them ideal for low-light applications. They offer excellent color reproduction and less noise. On the other hand, CMOS cameras are generally more affordable and consume less power, providing faster frame rates and easier integration. Both types serve specific needs in microscopy, catering to different research and industrial applications.</p></p>
