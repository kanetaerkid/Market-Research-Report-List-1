<p><p>In the calibration software market, there are two primary types: installed and cloud-based solutions. Installed calibration software is deployed locally on users' machines, providing direct control over data and operations while requiring maintenance and updates. Conversely, cloud-based calibration software is hosted on remote servers, allowing users to access it via the internet. This model offers flexibility, scalability, and automatic updates, making it easier for businesses to manage their calibration processes without the need for extensive IT resources.</p></p>
